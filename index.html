RoVo: Robust Voice Protection Against Unauthorized Speech Synthesis with Embedding-Level Perturbations

Introduction

With the rapid advancement of AI-based data generation technologies, speech synthesis models like Deep Voice enable the creation of highly realistic speech, even for phrases never spoken. While these technologies have promising applications, they pose significant risks, such as generating malicious content for phishing or fake news propagation. Traditional defense mechanisms, such as adversarial perturbation injection at the signal level, are easily bypassed by speech enhancement techniques, limiting their effectiveness.

ㅤTo address these challenges, we propose RoVo, a novel defense method that inserts perturbations into high-dimensional embedding vectors and reconstructs speech as defended audio during the reconstruction process. This approach enhances defense robustness and remains resilient against advanced speech enhancement models. Experimental results show that RoVo effectively reduces attack success rates (ASR) across various synthesis models while preserving high speech quality. Furthermore, user studies confirm that RoVo maintains naturalness and usability and demonstrates promising transferability to unseen models.

ㅤThe proposed method surpasses traditional defenses by preserving quality and usability while preventing misuse. This study contributes to the development of practical and reliable defense mechanisms to protect speech data in complex environments.

Model Overview

ㅤOverview of the proposed adversarial embedding defense mechanism. The system protects against unauthorized voice synthesis and downstream misuse such as vishing, fake news, and fraudulent verification, even in the presence of noise removal or speech enhancement techniques.

ㅤThe architecture of RoVo, the proposed voice protection framework. RoVo applies adversarial perturbations directly to the embedding space generated by the Neural Codec Encoder. The perturbed embeddings are processed by the Neural Codec Decoder to reconstruct the protected voice while maintaining naturalness.
